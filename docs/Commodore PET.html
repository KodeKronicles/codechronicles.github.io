The Commodore PET, introduced in 1977 by Commodore International, stands as a pioneering icon in the history of personal computing. Its all-in-one design, featuring a built-in keyboard, monitor, and cassette tape drive, represented a paradigm shift in user interaction with computers.

The PET's compact yet powerful architecture made it an ideal choice for both home users and educational institutions seeking to integrate computing into their curriculum.

With its user-friendly interface and versatile programming capabilities, the Commodore PET helped democratize computing, laying the foundation for the PC revolution that would follow in its wake.
