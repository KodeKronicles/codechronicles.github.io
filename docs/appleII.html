Launched in 1977 by Apple Computer, the Apple II was a groundbreaking personal computer designed by Steve Wozniak.

It was one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers, featuring a MOS Technology 6502 microprocessor at its core, color graphics, and an audio cassette interface for storage.

Its introduction marked a pivotal moment in computer history by making computing accessible to a larger audience, spurring a plethora of software and peripheral developments.

It fostered an environment of creativity and introduced numerous people to programming, gaming, and home computing, which shaped the industry's future.
