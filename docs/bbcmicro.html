The BBC Microcomputer System, or BBC Micro, was developed by Acorn Computers in 1981 as part of the BBC's Computer Literacy Project aimed at educating the British public in computing.

The system was notable for its robust build, high-quality graphics, and powerful programming capabilities in BBC BASIC language.

It played a crucial role in the education of a generation, providing students and teachers with a reliable platform for learning computing concepts and programming, and it supported a range of peripherals that further enhanced its utility in educational settings.
